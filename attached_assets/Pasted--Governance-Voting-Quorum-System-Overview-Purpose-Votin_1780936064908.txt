# Governance Voting & Quorum System Overview

## Purpose

Voting allows a community to make collective decisions, but
participation alone does not guarantee legitimacy. A governance system
should ensure that decisions are made by a sufficiently representative
portion of the voting community.

**Quorum** is the minimum amount of voting participation required for a
proposal to be considered valid. Without quorum, a small group of voters
could pass proposals that affect the entire protocol.

A proposal should generally pass two independent checks:

1.  Quorum Requirement --- enough voting power participated.
2.  Approval Requirement --- enough of that voting power supported the
    proposal.

The governance system should support multiple quorum and voting models
so protocols can choose the configuration that best matches their needs.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Quorum Types

## 1. Fixed Token Quorum

Requires a fixed amount of voting power to participate.

Example: - Quorum = 1,000,000 voting power - Total votes cast =
850,000 - Result: Proposal fails due to insufficient participation.

Pros: - Simple and predictable - Easy to understand

Cons: - Does not adapt as token supply changes

## 2. Percentage-Based Quorum

Requires participation equal to a percentage of total eligible voting
power.

Example: - Total voting power = 100,000,000 - Quorum = 4% - Required
participation = 4,000,000

## 3. Dynamic Quorum

Quorum adjusts automatically based on historical participation.

Example: - Average participation of last 10 proposals = 4,000,000 -
Multiplier = 75% - Quorum = 3,000,000

## 4. Participation Quorum

Counts all votes regardless of position.

Calculation: FOR + AGAINST + ABSTAIN

## 5. Approval Quorum

Requires a minimum amount of supporting votes.

Calculation: FOR votes only

## 6. For + Abstain Quorum

Counts supportive and neutral participation.

Calculation: FOR + ABSTAIN

## 7. Dual Quorum

Requires both: - Minimum participation - Minimum approval percentage

## 8. Veto Quorum

Proposal passes unless opposition reaches a specified threshold.

## 9. Tiered Quorum

Different proposal types require different quorum levels.

Example: - Standard Proposal: 1% - Small Treasury Spend: 3% - Large
Treasury Spend: 5% - Protocol Upgrade: 10% - Constitution Change: 20%

## 10. Time-Weighted Quorum

Quorum changes over the voting period.

## 11. Security / Constitutional Quorum

Critical governance actions require elevated participation requirements.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Voting Methods

## Single Choice

Voter selects one option.

## Basic Voting

FOR / AGAINST / ABSTAIN

## Approval Voting

Voters may select multiple options.

## Ranked Choice Voting

Voters rank options by preference.

## Weighted Voting

Voters distribute voting power across multiple options.

## Quadratic Voting

Voting cost increases quadratically, reducing dominance by large
holders.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Recommended Default Governance Configuration

-   Proposal threshold: 0.1% of total voting power
-   Participation quorum: 2--10%
-   Approval threshold: 50--66%
-   Higher quorum requirements for treasury, protocol upgrades, and
    constitutional changes
